## Getting Started

### Core Mechanics

**Resource Management:**
- **Manpower** - Recruits infantry and basic units
- **Munitions** - Upgrades weapons and abilities
- **Fuel** - Builds vehicles and tanks

**Territory Control:**
- Capture and hold **Strategic Points** to generate resources
- Connect points to your **HQ** for maximum efficiency
- Cut off enemy supply lines to starve their economy

**Cover System:**
- **Green Cover** (walls, sandbags) - Best protection
- **Yellow Cover** (craters, low walls) - Moderate protection
- **Red Cover** (open ground) - No protection, avoid at all costs
- **Negative Cover** (windows, some positions) - Increased damage taken

### Controls & Interface

**Essential Hotkeys:**
- **Q, W, E, R** - Unit abilities
- **T** - Retreat
- **Ctrl + Number** - Create control group
- **Number** - Select control group
- **Space** - Jump to last alert
- **Backspace** - Center on HQ

### Important Tips

> [!TIP]
> **Always use cover!** Units in green cover can survive 3-4x longer than units in the open. Position is everything in Company of Heroes.

- **Flank enemy positions** - Attack from multiple angles
- **Preserve veteran units** - They gain experience and become more effective
- **Use combined arms** - Infantry, tanks, and support units work best together
- **Retreat damaged units** - Don't let squads die unnecessarily
- **Capture resources early** - Economy wins games

This campaign follows Able Company from D-Day through the liberation of France, showcasing the American forces' push into occupied Europe.

**Objectives:**

**Medal Reward:** üèÖ **Expert Infantryman Badge**

**Strategy:**
- This is a tutorial mission introducing basic mechanics
- Use engineers to clear obstacles and build cover
- Flank machine gun nests - never attack them head-on
- Use smoke grenades to advance safely

- **Team Survival** - Keep key squads alive

## General Tips & Strategies

### Resource Management

**Economy Basics:**
- Capture **Strategic Points** early
- Connect points to HQ for full income
- Build **Observation Posts** on key points
- Cut enemy supply lines when possible
- Balance spending between units and upgrades

**Resource Priorities:**
- **Early Game:** Manpower for infantry and territory control
- **Mid Game:** Fuel for vehicles and armor
- **Late Game:** Munitions for abilities and upgrades

---

### Combat Tactics

**Infantry Combat:**
- **Always use cover** - green cover is best
- **Flank enemy positions** - attack from multiple angles
- **Focus fire** - concentrate on one enemy squad at a time
- **Retreat damaged units** - preserve veterancy
- **Use abilities** - grenades, smoke, suppression

**Armor Combat:**
- **Combined arms** - never send tanks alone
- **Infantry support** - protects tanks from AT infantry
- **Flanking** - rear armor is weaker
- **Repairs** - keep engineers with your armor
- **Target priority** - destroy AT threats first

**Defensive Tactics:**
- **Layered defenses** - multiple lines of resistance
- **Mines and wire** - slow enemy advances
- **Overlapping fields of fire** - support positions cover each other
- **Reserves** - keep units ready to counterattack
- **Fallback positions** - prepare secondary lines

---

### Unit Management

**Veterancy:**
- Veteran units are much more effective
- Preserve experienced squads
- Use retreat to save units
- Veteran units unlock special abilities

**Unit Composition:**
- **Infantry** - Capture territory, provide vision
- **Machine Guns** - Suppress and pin enemy infantry
- **Mortars** - Counter enemy machine guns and emplacements
- **AT Guns** - Destroy enemy armor
- **Tanks** - Breakthrough and exploitation
- **Engineers** - Build defenses, repair vehicles, clear obstacles

---

### Advanced Tactics

**Flanking:**
- Attack from multiple directions
- Force enemy to split attention
- Hit weak points in enemy lines
- Use speed and mobility

**Suppression:**
- Machine guns suppress enemy infantry
- Suppressed units move slowly and fight poorly
- Use suppression to pin enemies while flanking
- Mortars counter enemy machine guns

**Combined Arms:**
- Use all unit types together
- Infantry spots for tanks
- Tanks destroy fortifications
- Artillery softens defenses
- Engineers support the advance

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: Which campaign should I play first?
**A:** Start with the **US Campaign** (base game). It's the tutorial campaign and teaches core mechanics. Then play British and Panzer Elite campaigns from Opposing Fronts, and finish with the Tales of Valor mini-campaigns.

### Q: What difficulty should I choose?
**A:** 
- **Easy** - Learn mechanics and enjoy the story
- **Normal** - Balanced challenge, recommended for first playthrough
- **Hard** - Requires strong tactical skills
- **Expert** - For experienced RTS players only

### Q: How important is cover?
**A:** **Extremely important!** Units in green cover survive 3-4x longer than units in the open. Always position your units in cover and flank enemies to negate their cover.

### Q: Should I build defenses or attack?
**A:** It depends on the mission. Some missions are defensive (hold positions), others are offensive (capture objectives). Read the mission briefing and adapt your strategy.

### Q: How do I counter enemy tanks?
**A:**
- **AT Guns** - Powerful but immobile
- **Tank Destroyers** - Mobile AT platforms
- **Bazookas/Panzerschrecks** - Infantry AT weapons
- **Mines** - Cheap and effective
- **Flanking** - Attack rear armor with your own tanks

### Q: What's the best way to assault fortified positions?
**A:**
1. Use **artillery** or **mortars** to suppress defenders
2. Use **smoke** to advance safely
3. **Flank** from multiple directions
4. Use **tanks** to destroy bunkers and emplacements
5. Clear buildings with **grenades** and **flamethrowers**

### Q: How do I defend against enemy attacks?
**A:**
1. Build **defensive structures** - sandbags, wire, mines
2. Position **machine guns** to cover approaches
3. Place **AT guns** to counter enemy armor
4. Keep **reserves** ready to counterattack
5. Use **artillery** on enemy concentrations

### Q: Can I skip missions?
**A:** No, campaigns are linear. You must complete each mission to progress to the next.

### Q: Are there different endings?
**A:** No, each campaign has a single ending. However, your performance (medals earned) affects your overall rating.

### Q: What are medals and how do I earn them?
**A:** Medals are bonus objectives in missions. They're optional but provide additional challenges. Check mission objectives for medal requirements.

### Q: How long does each campaign take?
**A:**
- **US Campaign (Invasion of Normandy):** 12-15 hours
- **British Campaign (Liberation of Caen):** 8-10 hours
- **Panzer Elite Campaign (Operation Market Garden):** 7-9 hours
- **Tiger Ace Campaign:** 2-3 hours
- **Causeway Campaign:** 2-3 hours
- **Falaise Pocket Campaign:** 2-3 hours
- **Total:** 45-65 hours for all 6 campaigns

### Q: Can I replay missions?
**A:** Yes! You can replay any completed mission from the campaign menu to earn medals or improve your performance.

### Q: What's the difference between the factions?
**A:**
- **US Forces:** Versatile, strong infantry, good armor
- **British Forces:** Defensive, emplacements, heavy tanks
- **Panzer Elite:** Mobile, fast vehicles, tactical abilities
- **Wehrmacht (in skirmish):** Balanced, strong late-game armor

### Q: Are there any missable achievements?
**A:** The Steam version of Company of Heroes does not include achievements. Focus on enjoying the campaigns and earning medals!

### Q: Should I use a walkthrough on my first playthrough?
**A:** The US Campaign teaches you the basics. Try missions on your own first, and consult this guide if you get stuck. Learning through trial and error is part of the fun!

### Q: What happens after I complete all campaigns?
**A:** You can:
- Replay missions for medals
- Try higher difficulties
- Play **Skirmish mode** against AI
- Play **Multiplayer** (if servers are still active)
- Try **custom maps** from the community
