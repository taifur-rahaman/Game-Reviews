## Overview

Commandos: Behind Enemy Lines is the legendary real-time tactics game that defined the tactical stealth genre. Set during World War II, players take command of a small squad of elite Allied soldiers—each with unique and specialized skills—to complete dangerous covert missions behind enemy lines. Known for its punishing difficulty, isometric perspective, and detailed pre-rendered levels, it requires extreme planning, coordination, and split-second execution to slip past patrols, sabotage military targets, and escape unnoticed.

### Campaign Logs
*   **Supervisor:** Colonel Montague Smith

#### 🪖 Mission 1: Baptism of Fire (Norway) — Feb 20, 1941
*   **The Setup:** With Norway under German occupation since our retreat from Narvik last June, the Colonel tasked us with hitting them where it hurts. Our objective: destroy the Relay Tower in Stavanger to cripple their reinforcement capabilities in the Northern Sea Range.
*   **Squad:** Tiny (Green Beret), Fins (Marine), and Tread (Driver).
*   **The Run:**
    *   **The Infiltration:** We started scattered across the shore. The first order of business was regrouping. Fins swam out stealthily, using his diving gear, and grabbed an inflatable boat to ferry Tiny and Tread across the freezing waters.
    *   **Silent Killers:** Tiny is an absolute beast. He used his decoy to distract the patrols, crept up behind them, and handled them silently with his combat knife. The line-of-sight cones in this game are incredibly tight—one step inside the bright green zone and you're toast.
    *   **The Boom:** Once we cleared a path, Tiny placed explosives next to the fuel barrels by the relay station. The explosion was majestic, lighting up the Norwegian sky. We slipped away in the boat before the alarms could even settle.
*   **Obtained Rank:** Corporal — A solid start, but the Colonel expects perfection.

#### 🪖 Mission 2: A Quiet Blow-up (Norway) — March 1, 1941
*   **The Setup:** Operation Claymore is officially approved—our first medium-scale incursion over Norway. This time, we had to neutralize industrial fuel facilities and merchant ships in the Stamsund area of the Lofoten islands.
*   **Squad:** Tiny (Green Beret), Fins (Marine), Tread (Driver), plus Duke (Sniper) and Inferno (Sapper).
*   **The Run:**
    *   **Water Hazard:** The Storfjord camp was heavily fortified, and a German patrol boat with a machine gun turret kept cruising the waters. Fins had to time his crossings perfectly to avoid being shredded.
    *   **Duke's Precision:** Duke (the Sniper) was the MVP of the early phase. With extremely limited ammo, every shot had to count. He picked off key watchtower guards, allowing Tiny to carry and hide the bodies to prevent the barracks from sending reinforcements.
    *   **Inferno's Demolition:** Once the area was clear, Inferno (Sapper) did what he does best: set charges on the fuel depots in the northern camp.
    *   **The Getaway:** Tread hotwired a German truck, the whole squad piled in, and we punched through the southwest road block to extract safely.
*   **Obtained Rank:** Corporal — The difficulty spike here was real. Keeping five specialists alive and synchronized requires the patience of a saint.

### First Impressions
*   **The Genesis of Tactics:** It's fascinating to experience the grandfather of *Desperados*. The mechanics are purer and even more punishing, showing where the blueprint for real-time tactical stealth truly came from.
*   **Highly Unforgiving:** The line of sight and hearing systems are incredibly precise. One mistake triggers the alarm, making quicksaving my absolute best friend.
*   **Elite Specialists:** Managing the Green Beret, Sniper, Marine, and others feels like solving a complex, high-stakes puzzle where everyone must play their role perfectly.
